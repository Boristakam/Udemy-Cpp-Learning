A recursive function is a function that calls itself either directly or indirectly through another function (we end up with 2 or more activation records or stack frames for this function next to each other on the stack)
can lead to stack overflow errors if perfomed indefinitely ( infinite recursion )
resource limitations (non-functional requirements) can also mean that perfectly fine recursive solutions are changed to iterative solution (even though they are less elegant and harder to understand)
