& operator allows to access the address of any vars pointers included e.g. cout << &length // prints the address where length is stored

size of the pointer and the size of what it points to are not the same thing (therefore pointers are not what they point to, they are simple vars on their own)
pointer store/point to addresses, and they also have their own address as they are variables. 
all pointers in a program have the same size and may be pointing to very large or very small types (bytes)
    print sizeof(your declared pointer) to find out the size of a pointer for your machine.


typed pointers point to variable of specific type, so an integer pointer cannot point to/store the address of a double (creates compiler err)
